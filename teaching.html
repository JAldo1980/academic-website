Teaching

I am an experienced and dedicated teacher who is passionate about providing high-quality education to students from diverse backgrounds. Over the past five years, I have developed a comprehensive teaching style that encourages active learning and fosters an inclusive and engaging classroom environment.


In my classes, I prioritize open discussion and constructive debate while ensuring that students are exposed to a diverse range of perspectives. To achieve this, I use a variety of teaching materials, including readings from non-western scholars, foreign-language visuals with subtitles, and examples from outside the western world. I also encourage my students to explore and engage with countries they have not studied or visited before.


Experiences

I have experience teaching at both the University of Copenhagen and the University of Leeds. At the University of Copenhagen, I served as a convenor for the course "Promotional Politics in Asia (PRG)" during the 2020/2021 Spring semester. I designed and delivered lectures and seminars for a class of 44 students, which were fully conducted on Zoom.


At the University of Leeds, I worked as a teaching assistant for several courses, including "Comparative Politics," "International Politics," "An Introduction to Thinking Interculturally," and "Skills and Issues in Intercultural Studies."


In these roles, I provided small-group teaching and delivered seminars to undergraduate and postgraduate students. Additionally, I created a short digital contribution titled "Case study: Branding Thailand" for the online portion of the "An Introduction to Thinking Interculturally" course.


Moreover, I designed and delivered two guest lectures and seminars titled "Culture and Democracy Discourse" and "Nation Branding" for the "Skills and Issues in Intercultural Studies" course.
