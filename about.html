About

Hello, and welcome to my website. Here's a little bit more about me.

I am a Post-Doctoral Research Fellow in Leadership for Inclusive and Democratic Politics at the University of Birmingham.

My areas of expertise include democracy, authoritarianism, elections, environmental leadership, and nation branding. I have a focus on Southeast Asia and am fluent in Thai.

My latest publication is a co-authored article on the independence of election commissions in non-democratic regimes, using Thailand as an example. This paper is part of a larger election project funded by the United States Institute of Peace.

Along with my research work, I also host and was a former editor of the Nordic Asia Podcast series, where I interview other Asia researchers and specialists about their work and publications.

Please feel free to contact me

Biography

I joined the University of Birmingham in December 2021 after two years at the Nordic Institute of Asian Studies and the Department of Political Science at the University of Copenhagen, where I was a postdoctoral research fellow.

Prior to that I was a doctoral researcher at the University of Leeds. My PhD thesis was nominated for the British International Studies Association’s 2020 Michael Nicholson Thesis Prize and longlisted for the International Convention of Asia Scholars 2021 Book Prize for the Best Dissertation in the Social Sciences.

In 2020, I was selected a Young Southeast Asia Fellow (non-resident) by the Southeast Asia Research Group for my leading work in the study of Southeast Asia.
